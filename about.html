Francisco Valente Gonçalves

I was born in the serene village of Monforte-da-Beira, located in the district of Castelo Branco, Portugal, in 1980. I graduated with a degree in Portuguese Language and Culture from the University of Lisbon, in 2007. In that same institution, I taught Portuguese as foreign language to international students, while I attended a Master degree in Portuguese Language and Culture, from 2008 to 2009. From 2010 to 2015, I worked in Goa, India, as a teacher of Portuguese Language and Culture.

I began my musical studies in 1986, in the Conservatory of Castelo Branco, where I was part of the “Grupo the Câmara e Percussão Orff”, founded and directed by maestro Luis Cipriano. In 2002, in Lisbon, I resumed my musical studies by attending classical piano classes with pianist José Bon de Sousa. In “Academia Amadores de Música”, also based in Lisbon, I had the privilege to study Musical Analysis and Composition with great master Eurico Carrapatoso. In 2017, I enrolled myself in a degree in Music - Classical Guitar in Instituto Politécnico de Castelo Branco. After having participated in a masterclass by the Croatian guitarist Dejan Ivanović, I am presently studying under his guidance.

I always had a strong interest for hand-crafted works and, in my opinion, the respect and admiration for the beauty and hidden power of human hands should not be inferior to the one devoted to human mind. I believe this fascination derives from being the grand-son, from my father’s side, of a professional cabinet-maker and leisure-time folk guitar player, and from my mother’s side, of an elementary school teacher and also leisure-time hand-crafts artist (making different kinds of handicrafts using leather, wood and cork). Between 2018 and 2019, I took the opportunity to attend a guitar making course in the city of Castelo Branco. Since then, I have been researching about classical guitar construction and gradually set my own workshop in the laid-back interior village of Monforte-da-Beira, my home town. I made many of my own tools and I am glad to be making fully-handmade concert classical guitars in a traditional manner. I decided to honor the memory of both my grand-fathers’ by using their surnames on the labels of my guitars - “VALENTE GONÇALVES”.
